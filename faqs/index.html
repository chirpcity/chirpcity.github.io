FAQs

I have found an injured/dead bird. What do I do?

Do not attempt to rescue a raptor (hawk, owl) or a large bird such as a heron or a bittern. Contact your local wildlife conservancy or organization.
If the bird is injured, but still alive, put the bird in a dark box, such as a shoebox, to allow it to recuperate. Contact the nearest nature organization or museum, and they will likely provide options to turn in dead or injured birds. For more information, visit birdmoniters.net.

How is reporting the death of a bird helpful?

Reporting the death of a bird is helpful to see which species of bird is being most affected by window collisions.

What are the most common birds affected by window collisions?

The most common birds affects by window collisions are the white throated sparrow, brown creeper, and ovenbird. For more info go to birdmoniters.net.

Where can I donate to ChirpCity or their cause?

Currently, ChirpCity does not accept payments. Some good organizations to donate too that share the same interests are The Lincoln Park Zoo, The Chicago Academy of Sciences, The Field Museum and The Cornell Lab of Ornithology.
